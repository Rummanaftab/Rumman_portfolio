Research

M.Tech Thesis

📄 Master's Thesis  ·  KIIT University  ·  2024–2026

Crop Disease Classification Using Convolutional Neural Networks

SupervisorMr. Pushkar Kishore
InstitutionSchool of Computer Engineering, KIIT
DegreeM.Tech — CSE (AI/DS)
Roll No.24167015
DatasetPlantVillage (~20,000 images)

This thesis presents an automated system for crop disease classification using a novel hybrid Convolutional Neural Network. Addressing the limitations of manual disease detection — time-consuming, subjective, requiring expert knowledge — the proposed system leverages deep learning to identify Early Blight, Late Blight, and Healthy conditions in potato leaf images. The hybrid CNN architecture integrates Inception modules, depthwise separable convolutions, and residual connections, trained with the Adam optimizer on PlantVillage data. The system is further enhanced with an LLM-based advisory module providing farmers with actionable, natural-language treatment recommendations after classification.

97.33%Training Accuracy
89%Test Accuracy
87%Precision
86%F1-Score

🏗 Hybrid CNN Architecture

Combines Inception modules for multi-scale feature extraction, depthwise separable convolutions for computational efficiency, and residual connections to prevent vanishing gradients. Detects subtle patterns — texture changes, discoloration, lesion morphology — across multiple spatial scales.

🤖 LLM Advisory Module

An integrated Small Language Model (SLM) that generates context-sensitive treatment advice post-classification. Provides disease descriptions, symptoms, biological causes, and preventive strategies in natural language — making AI accessible to farmers without technical expertise.

📊 Data Pipeline

Preprocessed ~20,000 labeled PlantVillage images with corruption filtering, uniform 224×224 resizing, pixel normalization (0–1), and augmentation (rotation, CLAHE). Strategically split into training and validation subsets for overfitting prevention and robust evaluation.

⚡ Baseline Benchmarks

Outperformed five state-of-the-art lightweight models under identical experimental conditions. Hybrid CNN achieved 72.34% validation accuracy vs. EfficientNet's 15.04%, demonstrating vastly superior generalization.
